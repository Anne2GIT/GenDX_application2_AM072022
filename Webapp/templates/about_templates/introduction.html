Introduction
Before the age of 75, 1 in every 3 males and 1 in every 4 females is diagnosed with cancer. Despite the growing amount of researches into cancer, there is still little insight into this disease. That is why a project was initiated to deliver a tool which facilitates the identification and visualisation of differences in gene expression levels between cancer and non-cancer tissue to aid better understanding of this increasingly common disease.

Of the total number of ongoing cancer researches, only a small amount focuses on specific tissues. Tools to identify and visualize differential gene expression levels in infected tissues already exist, such as GEPIA and GTEx, which show the tissue specific gene expression and regulation. However those tools do not include a comparison between expression levels for gene families in cancer and non-cancer tissue, leaving room for a more specific approach. The tool that will be created will contain, besides the differential gene expression levels between cancer and non-cancer tissue for a selected few organ tissues, the option to analyse these gene expression levels on gene family and subfamily level, using interactive figures. In this way we try to support ongoing and future cancer researches.

Due to the large number of past and ongoing researches as described in Margaret I. Cuomo's book 'A world without cancer', “More than 40 years after the war on cancer was declared, we have spent billions fighting the good fight. The National Cancer Institute has spent some $90 billion on research and treatment during that time. Some 260 nonprofit organizations in the United States have dedicated themselves to cancer — more than the number established for heart disease, AIDS, Alzheimer’s disease, and stroke combined. Together, these 260 organizations have budgets that top $2.2 billion.” and thus data being generated, there is a vast amount of data and methods available for such gene expression analyses. As data source, the GDC Data portal, containing harmonized cancer datasets, will be used. To illustrate the value of this tool, the gene families occuring in a specific organ tissue will be selected for gene expression analysis.

The differential gene expression between cancer and non-cancer tissues are analysed and visualised using an algorithm and multiple plots. The tool can be used by a biological researcher for examining tissue specific gene expression on multiple levels, such as age and gender. The tool can aid in cancer diagnostics and further research for developing cancer therapies or medicine.
